## 1. BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)

Best for: SMB sales, shorter sales cycles, transactional deals.

### Grading Dimensions under BANT

| Dimension | What to Look For in Transcripts |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **Budget Discovery** | Did the rep ask about budget? Did they anchor pricing early or late? Did the prospect volunteer budget range? Did the rep handle "no budget" well? |
| **Authority Mapping** | Did the rep identify the decision-maker? Did they ask "who else is involved?" Did they try to get the economic buyer on a call? |
| **Need Identification** | Did the rep uncover a real business pain? Did they quantify the cost of inaction? Or did they just demo features? |
| **Timeline Establishment** | Did the rep ask when the prospect needs a solution? Did they create urgency? Or was there no timeline discussion at all? |

### BANT Grade Mapping
- **A**: Rep consistently uncovers all 4 BANT elements across calls, with probing follow-ups
- **B**: Rep covers 3 of 4 elements on most calls, with some depth
- **C**: Rep covers 1-2 elements inconsistently, surface-level questions
- **D/F**: Rep rarely qualifies — mostly just demos features

## 2. MEDDIC / MEDDPICC

Best for: Enterprise sales, complex deals, long sales cycles, multi-stakeholder environments.

### Grading Dimensions under MEDDIC

| Dimension | What to Look For |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Metrics** | Does the rep quantify the business impact? "How much is this costing you?" / "What would a 20% improvement mean in revenue?" |
| **Economic Buyer** | Has the rep identified who controls the budget? Have they gotten access to that person? |
| **Decision Criteria** | Does the rep know what the prospect will evaluate solutions on? Technical requirements, ROI threshold, integration needs? |
| **Decision Process** | Does the rep map the buying process? Procurement, legal review, board approval, pilot requirements? |
| **Identify Pain** | Is the pain clearly articulated? Is it compelling enough to drive change? Has the rep tied their solution to the specific pain? |
| **Champion** | Has the rep identified an internal advocate? Are they coaching that champion on how to sell internally? |

### MEDDPICC Additions
| **Paper Process** | Does the rep understand the contract/procurement process? Legal review timelines? |
| **Competition** | Does the rep know who else is being evaluated? How do they position against competitors? |

## 3. SPIN Selling

Best for: Consultative sales, reps who need to improve discovery depth.

### SPIN Dimensions

| Phase | What to Grade |
|-------|--------------|
| **Situation Questions** | Does the rep gather context? Company size, current tools, team structure? (Should be brief — don't over-index here) |
| **Problem Questions** | Does the rep uncover difficulties and dissatisfactions? "What's not working about your current approach?" |
| **Implication Questions** | Does the rep amplify the pain? "What happens if this problem continues?" / "How does this affect your team's ability to hit targets?" |
| **Need-Payoff Questions** | Does the rep guide the prospect to articulate the value of solving the problem? "If you could automate that, what would you do with the saved time?" |

### SPIN Grade Mapping
- Most reps over-index on Situation and under-invest in Implication and Need-Payoff
- Strong reps spend 60%+ of discovery time on Problem and Implication questions
- The shift from Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-Payoff should feel natural, not scripted

## 4. Challenger Sale

Best for: Reps selling to prospects who don't yet know they have a problem, or where differentiation is key.

### Challenger Dimensions

| Dimension | What to Grade |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Teach** | Does the rep bring an insight the prospect didn't know? A data point, trend, or reframe that changes how they think about their situation? |
| **Tailor** | Does the rep customize the message to the prospect's specific industry, role, and situation? Or is it one-size-fits-all? |
| **Take Control** | Does the rep drive the conversation and the process? Do they push back constructively when the prospect tries to derail? Do they hold firm on pricing? |

## 5. Custom Framework Builder

When the user wants a custom framework, collect these inputs:

1. **What are the 5-8 things that matter most to you on a sales call?** (e.g., "qualifying budget early," "multi-threading," "setting next steps")
2. **How would you weight them?** (equal weight, or some matter more?)
3. **What does 'good' look like for each?** (Example: "For qualification, good means they know budget, authority, and timeline by end of first call")

Then build a custom grading rubric that maps each user-defined dimension to observable behaviors in transcripts, using the same A-F letter grade scale.
