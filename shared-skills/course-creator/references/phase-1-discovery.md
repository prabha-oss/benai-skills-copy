# Phase 1: Discovery & Positioning

## Purpose
Nail down WHAT the course is, WHO it's for, and WHY it's different — before writing a single lesson.

---

## Step 1.1: Topic Extraction

- **Raw idea:** Ask the user to describe what they want to teach
- **YouTube URL:** Fetch transcript, extract and summarize key topics, frameworks, unique insights, quotable moments. Present summary and ask which topics to turn into modules.
- **Existing content:** Analyze and summarize what's there

**Output:** Topic summary + key themes

---

## Step 1.2: Ideal Student Profile

Ask/determine:
- Who specifically is this course for? (demographics, role, experience level)
- What is their current skill level with this topic?
- What are they struggling with RIGHT NOW?
- What have they already tried that didn't work?
- What's their motivation for learning this? (career, money, hobby, necessity)

**Propose 2-3 ideal student personas and let user pick/refine.**

**Example:**
> **Ideal Student:** Non-technical creators, entrepreneurs, or solopreneurs (age 25-55) who've heard about AI tools but feel overwhelmed. They may have tried ChatGPT but never built anything real. They want to use AI to save time and create tools for their business but don't know where to start.

---

## Step 1.3: Transformation Statement

Write the core promise using this formula:
> "By the end of this course, students will be able to [SPECIFIC MEASURABLE OUTCOME]."

**Propose 3 transformation statements, ranked by specificity. Let user pick.**

Rules for good transformation statements:
- Must be specific and measurable (not "understand AI" but "build 30 working projects")
- Must describe what the student CAN DO, not what they'll KNOW
- Must be achievable within the course timeframe
- Must be exciting enough to sell

**Example:**
> "By the end of this 30-day challenge, you will have built and shipped 30 real-world projects using Claude — from automations to full apps — even if you've never written a line of code."

---

## Step 1.4: Course Format Selection

Present this table and let user select:

| Format | Best For | Duration | Price Range | Completion Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Self-paced | Evergreen, passive income | 4-12 weeks | $50-$500 | 10-15% |
| Cohort-based | High transformation, premium | 4-8 weeks | $500-$5,000 | 85-90% |
| Challenge | Community building, momentum | 5-30 days | Free-$500 | 60-80% |
| Hybrid | Best of both worlds | 4-12 weeks | $200-$2,000 | 40-60% |

Adjust all subsequent phases based on format choice.

---

## Step 1.5: Duration & Pacing

Based on format, propose duration options:
- **Challenges:** 5, 7, 14, 21, or 30 days
- **Cohort:** 4, 6, 8, or 12 weeks
- **Self-paced:** Estimated total hours (5h, 10h, 20h, 40h+)

Define pacing:
- Lessons per day/week
- Time commitment per lesson (aim for under 15 min for video, under 10 min ideal)
- Time commitment for activities/exercises

---

## Step 1.6: Unique Positioning Angle

Research what already exists on this topic (use web search if available). Then propose 3 positioning angles using:

**Formula:** "[OUTCOME] using [UNIQUE METHOD] for [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE] in [TIMEFRAME]"

The angle should answer: "Why THIS course over the 100 others on this topic?"

**Example:**
> "Not another 'intro to AI' course. This is a daily build challenge — you ship something real every single day using Claude's most powerful features: skills, projects, MCP, and computer use. No fluff. No theory lectures. Just building."
