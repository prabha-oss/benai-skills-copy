# Phase 3: Deep Discovery

Generate DYNAMIC questions based on the cloned site's sections and the user's business context from Phase 1. These are NOT prefixed templates — you create them specifically for THIS business.

---

## Part 1: How to Generate Questions

### 1.1 Read the Cloned Site's Structure

Look at the sections you built in Phase 2. For each section, determine what content needs to be replaced with the user's real information.

### 1.2 Use Phase 1 Context

The user's answers to Q1-Q7 tell you:
- What type of business (Q1) → shapes what questions are relevant
- What they offer (Q3) → shapes how specific you can be
- Who their customer is (Q4) → shapes the angle of questions
- What problem they solve (Q5) → shapes depth of pain-point questions
- What proof they have (Q6) → shapes social proof questions
- What CTA they want (Q7) → shapes conversion questions

### 1.3 Question Generation Rules

1. **One question per message** via `AskUserQuestion`
2. **Be specific to the business** — don't ask "What's your value prop?" Ask "You said you help [Q4] with [Q3]. What's the biggest transformation a client experiences after working with you?"
3. **Reference their own words** — use language from Phase 1 answers
4. **Skip what you already know** — don't re-ask Q1-Q7 content
5. **Group by section** — ask all questions for one section before moving to the next
6. **Stop when you have enough** — you need enough to write compelling copy, not a biography

---

## Part 2: Question Types by Section

For each section type on the cloned site, here are the KINDS of questions to ask. Adapt the wording to the user's specific business.

### Hero Section
- What's the biggest result/transformation a client gets? (for the headline)
- What would your dream client say your service does in one sentence? (for the subtitle)

### Features / Services Section
- What are your top 3-4 specific capabilities/services/features?
- For each: what's the benefit to the customer (not just the feature)?

### Problem / Pain Section
- What's happening in a prospect's life right before they look for your solution?
- What have they tried before that didn't work?
- What's the cost of NOT solving this problem?

### Testimonials / Social Proof Section
- Can you paste 2-3 of your best testimonials? Include the person's name and role.
- Any specific numbers or results you can share? (e.g., "3x revenue", "$50K saved")
- Which client logos can we display?

### Process / How It Works Section
- Walk me through what happens after someone signs up / books a call / buys.
- How many steps? What's the timeline?

### Pricing Section
- Do you want to show pricing? If yes, what are your tiers/packages?
- What's included in each tier?
- Which tier do you want to highlight as recommended?

### FAQ Section
- What are the top 3 questions or objections prospects ask before buying?
- What concerns do they have about price, timeline, or results?

### CTA / Contact Section
- What form fields do you need? (name, email, company, phone, message?)
- Where should submissions go? (email, CRM, Calendly?)
- Any urgency element? (limited spots, deadline, special offer?)

---

## Part 3: When to Stop Asking

You have enough when you can confidently write:
- A compelling headline and subtitle for the hero
- Copy for every section on the cloned site
- Real testimonials or social proof (if they have any)
- Specific CTAs with the right form fields

**Typical question count:** 8-15 dynamic questions depending on section count and business complexity.

**Present a summary** after all questions:

```
Great — I now have everything I need to customize your site. Here's what I'll be writing:

HERO: [headline angle based on their transformation]
[SECTION]: [what content goes here]
[SECTION]: [what content goes here]
...

Ready to start customizing? I'll go section by section, showing you 3 options for each headline.
```

Wait for confirmation before proceeding to Phase 4.
