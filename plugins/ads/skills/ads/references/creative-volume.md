#### Andromeda Algorithm (2024-2026)
Meta's Andromeda deep-learning delivery system fundamentally changed creative strategy:
- **"Creative is the targeting"** — instead of manual audience definition, advertisers create ads for different customer personas and let AI match content to users
- Andromeda **groups conceptually similar creatives** together, treating minor variations as the same ad. The algorithm needs **conceptually different creatives** to properly segment audiences
- Loading 20+ nearly-identical variations in one ad set is counterproductive. Diversity of **angle, format, persona, and message** matters more than raw quantity
- Advertisers report **22% increase in ROAS** when using AI-driven targeting with diverse creative

#### Meta Refresh Cadence
| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Frequency (prospecting, 7d) | >3.0 | Signals over-saturation — new creative or audience |
| CTR decline | >20% over 14 days | Refresh creative immediately |
| Creative age | >3-4 weeks | 29% higher CPMs, 35% CTR drop expected |
| Top brand average refresh | Every 10.4 days | Benchmark for high-performing accounts |
| Hard ceiling | 90 days | Replace regardless of performance (M-CR5 check) |
| Users seeing same ad 6-10 times | — | 4.1% less likely to purchase vs 2-5 times |

### Google Performance Max

| Asset Type | Minimum | Recommended | Maximum (per asset group) | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Images (landscape + square + portrait) | 5 | 15-20 | 20 total | Fill ALL slots for "Excellent" Ad Strength |
| Logos (landscape + square) | 1 | 3-5 | 5 total | Both landscape (4:1) and square (1:1) |
| Videos (landscape + square + vertical) | 1 | 3-5 | 5 (testing expansion to 15 in 2026) | Native video in 16:9, 1:1, 9:16 |
| Short headlines (30 chars) | 3 | 10-15 | 15 | Unique, keyword-relevant, diverse angles |
| Long headlines (90 chars) | 1 | 3-5 | 5 | |
| Descriptions (90 chars) | 2 | 4-5 | 5 | 1 must be under 60 chars |

#### Ad Strength Impact
- **"Excellent" Ad Strength** = ~12-15% more conversions than "Poor"
- Achieving "Excellent" requires filling most or all asset slots
- Google rates: uniqueness of headlines (Levenshtein distance), keyword inclusion, format coverage
- 3-7 headlines = Poor/Average, 8-10 = Good, 11-15 = Excellent

#### Google PMax Refresh Cadence
| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Asset labeled "Low" performing | After 3-4 weeks | Swap with new creative |
| Quarterly baseline | Every 3 months | Review all assets, replace underperformers |
| Ad Strength < "Good" | Immediately | Add assets until "Excellent" |
| Video limit expansion | Testing in 2026 | 5 → 15 videos per asset group |

## Key 2025-2026 Trends

1. **Creative diversity over volume** — every platform's algorithm now clusters similar creatives. Conceptually different ads outperform 50 minor variations
2. **AI-driven creative optimization is default** — Meta Andromeda, Google PMax AI, TikTok Smart+, LinkedIn AI insights all assume diverse asset pools
3. **Shorter creative lifespans** — TikTok: 3-5 days fatigue onset; Meta: top brands refresh every 10 days; even Google PMax needs quarterly asset rotation
4. **Video is increasingly required** — Google PMax auto-generates if absent; TikTok is video-only; Meta's best formats are Reels/video; LinkedIn video growing
5. **Asset-level performance reporting** — Google and Microsoft now rate individual assets (Low/Good/Best), enabling data-driven rotation
